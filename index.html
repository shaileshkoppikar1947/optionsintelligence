🔄 Roll Advisor
What is the Roll Advisor?
When a covered call or put is about to expire or is being tested (stock approaching your strike), you face a decision: let it expire, get assigned, or "roll" — buy it back and sell a new one further out.

The Roll Advisor calculates the math for each scenario using your adjusted cost basis as the anchor. It tells you whether rolling generates enough credit to be worth it, or whether assignment is actually the better outcome given your real break-even.

📈 Wheel Cycle Tracker
What is the Wheel Cycle Tracker?
The wheel strategy runs in cycles: sell a cash-secured put → get assigned shares → sell covered calls → shares get called away → repeat. Each cycle has a definable start, middle, and end — with premium collected at every stage.

This view shows you the complete timeline of each position: when you started, how many cycles you've completed, total premium per cycle, and your cumulative adj CB reduction over time. It turns your trade history into a story.
⚖️ Position Sizing Calculator
What is Position Sizing?
One of the most overlooked aspects of the wheel strategy is how many contracts to run on each position. Too few and you leave income on the table. Too many and a single bad move wipes out months of premium.

This calculator uses your total portfolio value, each position's adj cost basis, and a maximum risk percentage you set to determine the mathematically optimal number of contracts per position. It also flags positions where you may be over- or under-allocated relative to your risk tolerance.
🎯 Strike Quality Score
Analyzes every trade you've made to score the quality of your strike decisions. Good strike = sold above your adjusted cost basis, kept premium, didn't need to buy back at a loss. Poor strike = sold below adj CB, or bought back for more than collected.
📊 Performance Benchmark
What is the Benchmark?
Every wheel trader eventually asks: "Is all this work actually beating just buying and holding the S&P 500?" The honest answer requires math most brokers will not do for you.

This benchmark calculates your wheel strategy yield — premium collected as a percentage of your cost basis — and compares it against S&P 500 returns over the same period. It also breaks down your total return into three components: unrealized stock appreciation, realized premium income, and cost basis reduction. You'll finally know if the wheel is worth it for your specific portfolio.
